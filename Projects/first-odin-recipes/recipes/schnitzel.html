Spätzle - mit Soß'

Spätzle is a really famous German food, espacilly in the southern part - mostly in swabia where it also was inveted.
It also can be eaten with Cheese and then you could say it's kinda like Mac And Cheese but It's waaay better.
The big difference between Spätzle and noodles is that its made with eggs.
Spätzle is usually just a side dish to a good roast venison etc. But if you are lazy and are looking for something delicous you may just eat it with gravy sauce. YUMMY!

It takes no Time and no Effort...

Prep Time: 10 mins

Cook Time: 10 mins

Total Time: 20 mins

Servings: 2

Yield: 2 servings

Ingredients

  • ½ cup all-purpose flour, plus more as needed
  • 1 large egg
  • ½ teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 pinch cayenne pepper
  • 1 tablespoon creme fraiche, sour cream, or plain yogurt
  • 3 tablespoons milk, or as needed
  • 1 package of Gravy Sauce

Directions

  1. Place flour, egg, salt, cayenne, crème fraîche, and milk in a mixing bowl. Whisk together until batter drips slowly off the whisk.
    If batter seems too thin, add a bit more flour; if too thick, add a bit more milk. You can test the thickness using the smooth side of a cheese grater with fairly large holes.
    If a dollop of the batter does not drip through the grating holes, it's the right consistency.

  2. Bring a pot of salted water to a simmer over medium-high heat.
    Use a spatula to push a spoonful of batter through the holes of the smooth side of the grater into the simmering water.

  3. When dumplings rise to the surface of the water, they are done.
    This will take just a few minutes. Remove dumplings with a slotted spoon, and repeat in batches with remaining batter.
